Introduction

D-Locks Ltd ("we," "our," or "us") employs cookies and other similar tracking technologies on our website. This policy describes how these tools function, why we employ them, and how you can control your preferences. Our practice complies with the UK's General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and other relevant privacy regulations.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are little text files that are deposited on your device when you access a website. They improve the user experience by recalling preferences, allowing core functions, and providing insights into how individuals are using the website. Certain cookies are important for the website to operate effectively, while others assist with analytics and marketing campaigns.

Types of Cookies We Use

We group our cookies into the following categories:

1. Essential Cookies – These are necessary for the site to work. They enable security, navigation, and accessibility.

2. Performance Cookies – These assist us in understanding how users use our site by gathering anonymous information on page visits, duration, and any problems that may be encountered.

3. Functional Cookies – These enable us to remember your settings, like language or region, to tailor your experience.

4. Marketing Cookies – These could be placed by third-party providers to serve you relevant adverts and monitor how our content is interacted with.

Managing Your Preferences

When you arrive at our site for the first time, you will be presented with a cookie banner requesting permission. You can change your preferences at any point via our cookie settings page. Most web browsers also enable you to manage or block cookies within their settings.

Third-Party Cookies

Third-party services can sometimes place cookies. These include, for example, analytics providers and embedded content from other platforms. We suggest looking at their privacy policies to see how they use data.

Cookies' Storage Period

Cookies can either be session cookies (removed once you close your browser) or persistent cookies (stored for a specified amount of time). It depends on the type of cookie and its usage.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you are entitled to:

  • Withdraw consent for non-essential cookies at any time.
  • Request information about the data collected using cookies.
  • Request data deletion where relevant.
